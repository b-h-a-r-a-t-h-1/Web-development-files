Numerical methods

The limitations of analytical methods have led the engineers to evolve graphical methods. numerical methods can be derived which are more accurate with the advent of high speed digital computers and increasing demand for numerical answers to various problems. Numerical approach enables solution of a complex problem with a great number (but) of very simple operations . The major advantage of numerical methods over analytical methods is that they are easily implementable with modern day computers and provide solutions in quick time as compared to analytical methods.

Linear equations

Direct method:

  1. Gauss jordon method

    This is modification of Gauss elimination method. In this method, elimination of unkowns is performed not in equations below but in equations above also, ultimately "Reducing the system to diagonal matrix form" i.e., each equation involving only one unknown'

  2. Gauss elimination method

    In this method,the unknowns are eliminated successively and the system is reduced to upper triangular system from which the unknowns are found by back substitution.

Indirect method:

  1. jacobi's iteration method

    iteration method are used for system of linear equations in which the coefficients of leading diagonal are large compared to others. In this method the initial approximation for solving the equations are values of variables are zero.,i.e for linear equation with three variable(x,y,z) are x=y=z=0. iteration is continued till the values of variables in previous iteration and current iteration are approximately equal

  2. gauss seidal method

    In this method the initial approximation for solving the equations are values of variables are zero.,i.e for linear equation with three variable(x,y,z) are x=y=z=0. For the next iteration we consider the values for the unkonown variable is consider as the values of the prvious iteration and "current iteration" also iteration is continued till the values of variables in previous iteration and current iteration are approximately equal

Eigen value

power method

    It is used to solve eigen value problems .in some applications, it is required to compute numerically largest eigen value and its corresponding eigen vector. it is also known as Rayleigh's power method.
